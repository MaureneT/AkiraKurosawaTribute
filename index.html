Akira Kurosawa

To be an artist means never to avert one's eyes

Biography

Akira Kurosawa is renowned Japanese filmaker who has directed more than 30 films in a span of 57 years. Many of his works were met with international acclaim, such as the Seven Samurai, an epic historical drama, and Rashomon, a psychological thriller that delves into the meaning of justice. Kurosawa's dynamic cinematography and striking imagery fueled his films with emotional intimacy. His films, Drunken Angel and Ikiru, displayed humanism at its finest through the gritty drama of living in the outskirts of society. His works were influential to a new generation of directors in the 20th century. He was not a just a filmmaker who produced one masterpiece, but several.

Timeline

  • 1910 Born on March 23, 1910 in Tokyo, Japan
  • 1936 Became director in training for the film studio, Photo Chemical Laboratories (which later became the major studio, Toho)
  • 1942 Began working on Sanshiro Sugata, his first film as director
  • 1944 Shot the propaganda film, Most Beautiful, which featured wartime female factory workers
  • 1945 Married Yoko Yaguchi, the leading actress of Most Beautiful
  • 1945 His son, Hisao, was born
  • 1945 Wrote the script for The Men Who Tread on the Tiger's Tale, a period drama film
  • 1946 Directed No Regrets for Our Youth, his first postwar film
  • 1954 His daughter, Kazuko, was born
  • 1959 Established the Kurosawa Production Company, with Toho as it's major shareholder
  • 1965 Released Red Beard, his last film to star Toshiro Mifune
  • 1966 The end of his exclusive contract with Toho
  • 1980 Released Kagemusha, an epic about a thief that was hired to become a double. It was financed by 20th Century Fox, received international acclaim and won the Palme d'Or award at the Cannes Film Festial
  • 1985 His wife, Yoko Yaguchi, passed away
  • 1985 Released Ran, a film based on William Shakespeare's King Lear
  • 1990 Received the Academy Award for Lifetime Achievement
  • 1998 Date of death
